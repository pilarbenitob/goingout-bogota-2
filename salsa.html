Salsa

Puerto Rico

Origin

The origin of salsa dates back to the 16th century in Cuba, where the Hispanic music brought by the Spanish conquerors and the music brought by the African slaves were mixed giving rise to a great variety of musical rhythms. These Afro-Cuban rhythms include: the Cuban son, the chachachá, the mambo, the son montuno, the guaracha, the guaguancó, and the charanga; but it is the Cuban Son that constitutes the musical basis of what is nowadays known as salsa.
